Programs — Disaster Preparedness & Response

Curriculum designed for Schools & Colleges: modular, practical, and aligned to safety best-practices.

Curriculum Modules

Each module contains learning objectives, suggested activities, assessment ideas and resources.

  • Learning outcomes: Identify common natural & man-made hazards; distinguish hazard/risk/vulnerability.

    • Activities: local hazard walk, hazard bingo, classroom mapping.
    • Assessment: short quiz & reflective map submission.

  • Learning outcomes: Describe mitigation strategies at household and school level.

    • Activities: risk assessment of classroom, simple retrofitting ideas.
    • Assessment: group project proposing low-cost mitigation for school.

  • Exercises on interpreting alerts and role-play of warning dissemination.

    • Assessment: drill evaluation & communication plan write-up.

  • Practical demonstrations and competency checks. Partner with local health services where possible.

    • Assessment: practical checklist & scenario-based test.

  • Plan, rehearse and evaluate evacuation routes for different hazards.

    • Assessment: timed evacuation drill & route audit.

  • Students and staff co-create an SDMP tailored to their campus.

    • Assessment: submitted SDMP and tabletop exercise.

  • Guidance for teachers and student leaders on supporting emotional recovery.

    • Assessment: scenario responses and resource list.

  • Emphasis on safety: do not put untrained students at risk. Invite professionals for demos.

  • Guidelines for planning recovery of learning and infrastructure.

  • Project-based learning: community awareness campaign or mock drill coordination.

  • Include local climate trends and adaptation examples.

  • Intro to simple mapping tools and smartphone reporting (ethics & safety).

  • Overview of relevant legal frameworks and compliance steps.

  • Students prepare short presentations on local incidents and lessons learned.

  • Tools for tracking student progress and issuing certificates.
