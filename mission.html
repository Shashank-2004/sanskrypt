Our Mission: Building Disaster Readiness in Schools and Colleges

Creating a culture of preparedness, safety, and resilience for every student, teacher, and administrator.
Disasters do not wait for a warning bell. Earthquakes, floods, fires, pandemics, and man-made hazards can strike at any time, disrupting lives and causing irreparable harm. Among the most vulnerable groups during such emergencies are students in schools and colleges. With classrooms full of young learners, teachers, and staff, educational institutions carry the immense responsibility of safeguarding lives while continuing to nurture knowledge.

Our mission is clear: to create a culture of disaster preparedness, safety, and resilience within schools and colleges. We aim to ensure that every student, teacher, and administrator is not only aware of potential risks but also empowered with the knowledge and skills to respond effectively.

Why Disaster Readiness in Schools and Colleges Matters

Educational institutions are second homes for students. On average, children and young adults spend more than one-third of their day at school or college. Any disaster that strikes during school hours can put hundreds—even thousands—of lives at risk.

A fire in a chemistry lab, if unmanaged, can endanger an entire block of classrooms.
An earthquake during school hours can cause panic, stampedes, and injuries if evacuation routes are unclear.
A flood in low-lying areas can disrupt classes for weeks, affecting both safety and education.

Tragedies in the past, such as the Kumbakonam School Fire (Tamil Nadu, 2004), where 94 children lost their lives, highlight the urgent need for readiness. These incidents remind us that preparedness is not a choice but a duty.
